Photographs — the specific rules

When you upload a photograph to build a venue, you are publishing someone's photograph to other people. So:

  • The photograph must be yours, or you must have permission to use it. Do not upload photographs you found on the internet, in a magazine, on a fishery's website, or in someone else's social media.
  • Do not upload photographs containing identifiable people unless they have agreed to it. Never upload a photograph containing a child.
  • Strip out anything private: number plates, house names, addresses, signage with personal details, phone numbers.
  • Respect the fishery. If a water is private, or its owner would not want it recreated and published, do not publish it. Consider whether you would be happy to show the venue owner what you have made.

8. Records and leaderboards

Swimcraft has venue, national and world records. When one of your catches sets a record, the following becomes public: your angler name, the species, the weight, the venue, the peg, and the image of the fish. Your wider catch history stays private to you. This is set out in full in our Privacy Policy.

Records are derived from an append-only ledger of catches, so they cannot be edited or forged. We may remove a record — and the account behind it — if we have good reason to believe it was obtained by cheating.

9. Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time. We will delete your account, your email address, your catch history and your personal data within 30 days.

One exception, and we would rather tell you now than surprise you later: if you hold a record, deleting your account does not erase the record. Your name is removed and the record is re-attributed to "Deleted angler". The fish, the weight, the venue and the peg stay on the board. A record that simply vanished would quietly promote someone else to a record they never actually beat, and the boards would stop being true.

Venues you published are content other players may be fishing. On deletion we will anonymise your creator credit unless you ask us to remove the venue instead — just tell us which you would prefer.
