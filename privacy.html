Privacy Policy

Swimcraft collects a deliberately small amount of data — mostly your gameplay, not personal information. This policy explains what we collect, why, who processes it, how long we keep it, and your rights under UK data protection law (the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018).

1. Who we are

Swimcraft is a fishing simulation game developed and published by Harbledown Digital ("we", "us", "our"), a sole trader business operated by Elliott Lee in the United Kingdom. We are the data controller responsible for your personal data when you play Swimcraft.

2. What data we collect

Account & identity

  • Your Steam identity. You sign in through Steam. We receive a Steam identifier linked to an internal player profile. We do not see or store your Steam password, email, or payment details — these stay with Valve/Steam.
  • Display name — the name you choose to show on your profile, catches, and leaderboards. This is public to other players.
  • Profile preferences — your chosen specialism and unit preferences (e.g. lb/oz vs metric).

Gameplay data (tied to your player profile)

  • Catch records — species, weight, venue, swim, date/time, and the tackle used (rod, bait, method, float, hook size, line).
  • Venues you create — venue names, descriptions, locations you enter, the photographs you upload to build venues, depth/geometry maps, water conditions, pegs, and fish stocking data.
  • Records & leaderboard entries — your personal bests and any venue records you set. Your display name is shown against records you hold (this is public, as leaderboards are by nature).
  • Named specimens — names you give to individual fish, which may be shown to other players who catch them.

Photographs

When you create a venue, you upload a photograph of a real location. These become part of the playable venue and may be visible to other players who fish that venue. Please do not upload photographs containing identifiable people or private information you don't want shared.

Technical data

We keep technical data to a minimum. We do not log connection data such as IP addresses for tracking or profiling. Our backend provider (Supabase) processes the technical information necessary to operate the database and authentication service, but we do not maintain separate connection or analytics logs.

In-game chat / community features

In-game text chat and community sharing features are planned for a future Early Access update and are not active in the current version. We do not currently collect or store chat messages. When these features launch, this policy will be updated, and chat will be filtered for profanity.

Website email sign-up (optional)

If you choose to sign up for updates on our website, we collect the email address you provide, solely to send occasional updates about Swimcraft. This is separate from the game. You can unsubscribe or ask us to delete your email at any time.

What we do NOT collect: we do not collect your real name, postal address, or payment information. We only collect an email address if you voluntarily submit one for updates. We do not track you across other sites, and we do not sell your data.

4. Who we share it with

We use trusted third parties to run Swimcraft. These act as our data processors:

  • Valve Corporation (Steam) — authentication and platform services. Your use of Steam is also governed by Valve's own privacy policy.
  • Supabase — our backend database, storage, and authentication infrastructure (hosted in the EU — Frankfurt, Germany), which stores your player profile, gameplay data, records, and venue photographs.
  • Formspree — processes the optional email sign-up form on our website. Applies only to the website, not the game.

Other players can see the parts of your data that are inherently public in an online records game: your display name, your leaderboard entries and records, and any venues or named specimens you create and share.

5. International transfers

Your data is stored and processed within the European Union (Frankfurt, Germany). The United Kingdom recognises the EU as providing an adequate level of data protection (an "adequacy decision"), so transfers of your data from the UK to the EU are permitted and protected under UK data protection law. We do not transfer your personal data outside the UK or EU.

6. How long we keep it

  • Account, profile, and gameplay records: retained while your account is active. If you request deletion, we will remove your profile and associated records within 30 days.
  • Chat messages: not applicable — in-game chat is not active, and no chat messages are collected or stored.
  • Technical data: we do not retain connection logs.
  • Website email sign-ups: retained until you unsubscribe or ask us to delete your email.

7. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict or object to processing of your data, to data portability, and to withdraw consent where processing is based on it. You can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

To exercise any of these, contact us at info@harbledowndigital.com. We will respond within one month.

8. Children

Swimcraft is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

9. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including authentication through Steam and access controls on our Supabase backend. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information and will notify you and the ICO of any breach as required by law.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the game develops (for example, as new features roll out during Early Access). We'll post the updated policy here and update the "last updated" date. Significant changes will be communicated in-game or via our Steam page.
